--- layout: track title: Precision Agriculture for Smallholder Farmers subtitle: How can we increase smallholder yields through precision agriculture? icon_path: /assets/images/agri-icon.png track_description: This is a great description. intro_slides: - question: What is disruptive technology doing for agriculture? answer: Precision Agriculture uses sensors, Big Data and digitized farming equipment to enable farmers to observe, measure and respond to variabilities in their environment (e.g. climate, soil composition, crop practices and disease). With this technology, farmers around the world are able to conserve valuable resources, such as water and fertilizer, while increasing both quality and quantity of their yields. - question: Why do we need more inclusive solutions? answer: At present, precision agriculture is largely out of the reach of smallholder farmers. Individual services to inform farmers of weather patterns, soil structure, agricultural practices etc. exist, but are mostly isolated. Moreover, farmers have difficulty implementing the recommendations since the often lack access to better inputs, machinery or markets. Making precision agriculture and the required inputs available to farmers could magnify their yields and earnings, while saving resources and conserving the environment. - question: Who would we like to attend? answer: At ii2030, we’ll bring together list: - item: A social entrepreneur using smartphone applications to provide smallholders with more accurate farm information and access to other actors in the agricultural ecosystem. - item: An NGO working locally with smallholder farmers to increase their business capacity and skills. - item: A major company developing digitized and smart farming machinery and equipment. - item: Agricultural product off-takers, who require end-to-end traceability to know exactly what went into production. - item: The head of a smallholder farmer cooperative, who can advise the team on the needs and usability of the solution. - question: How will we create impact? answer: At the conclusion of the workshop in Berlin, the team will walk away with a concrete strategy to make digital farming affordable and accessible to smallholder farmers. The ii2030 online platform will continue to be used by the team following the workshop, to develop a prototype and test the solution with smallholder farmers in the field, with guidance and support from Endeva. Together, the team will implement a solution that will drive economic growth and impact not only for smallholder farmers and agribusiness, but for all those affected by hunger and malnutrition. tour_slides: - img_path: /assets/images/conf-pic-4.jpg text: Our tour guides will take participants on an exploration of Berlin’s buzzing inclusive innovation scene to provide inspiration and information for each track. - img_path: /assets/images/conf-pic-5.jpg text: The 4-hour tour will take participants to 3 visionary organizations, where hosts will lead a discussion on how greater inclusion in the agriculture sector will impact society in 2030. - img_path: /assets/images/conf-pic-6.jpg text: The day will end with a “Meet & Greet” networking dinner.
