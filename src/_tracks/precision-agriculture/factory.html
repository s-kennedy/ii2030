--- layout: factory title: Precision Agriculture for Smallholder Farmers subtitle: How can we increase smallholder yields through precision agriculture? icon_path: /assets/icons/agri-red.svg track_description: This is a great description. intro_slides: - question: What is disruptive technology doing for agriculture? answer: Precision Agriculture uses sensors, Big Data and digitized farming equipment to enable farmers to observe, measure and respond to variabilities in their environment (e.g. climate, soil composition, crop practices and disease). With this technology, farmers around the world are able to conserve valuable resources, such as water and fertilizer, while increasing both quality and quantity of their yields. - question: Why do we need more inclusive solutions? answer: At present, precision agriculture is largely out of the reach of smallholder farmers. Individual services to inform farmers of weather patterns, soil structure, agricultural practices etc. exist, but are mostly isolated. Moreover, farmers have difficulty implementing the recommendations since the often lack access to better inputs, machinery or markets. Making precision agriculture and the required inputs available to farmers could magnify their yields and earnings, while saving resources and conserving the environment. - question: Who would we like to attend? answer: At ii2030, weâ€™ll bring together list: - item: A social entrepreneur using smartphone applications to provide smallholders with more accurate farm information and access to other actors in the agricultural ecosystem. - item: An NGO working locally with smallholder farmers to increase their business capacity and skills. - item: A major company developing digitized and smart farming machinery and equipment. - item: Agricultural product off-takers, who require end-to-end traceability to know exactly what went into production. - item: The head of a smallholder farmer cooperative, who can advise the team on the needs and usability of the solution. - question: How will we create impact? answer: At the conclusion of the workshop in Berlin, the team will walk away with a concrete strategy to make digital farming affordable and accessible to smallholder farmers.
